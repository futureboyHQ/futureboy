Pablo the shaman is on another plane

While driving back from my ballet class yesterday, I asked myself what kept me going at it passionately for the last 14 years.

I realize now that the constant need to push myself harder and fight against my weak mind have been the motivation. The moment you decide to improve (lift your leg higher, jump higher, keep your balance…) you enter a physical and mental journey. Physically, ballet is extremely demanding, which makes it mentally difficult. When your back contracts so much that it hurts and your toes are all squeezed out in your point shoes, your mind will demand you to quit. But when you push through and finally reach this beautiful arabesque, you realized that listening to your weak thoughts will bring you no where, neither in ballet nor in life.

I believe that this principle applies to all of us, in any field. The experts struggled and worked so hard to achieve what we admire them for. Never forget this. Excellence comes with resilience, drive, and the ability to overcome weak thoughts. Let’s fight against these “it is to hard” and “I’ll never be as good as them” reflections that bring us down. Yes it requires more effort, but believe me, we can only be at our true potential if we push ourselves further than our mental comfort zone.

We can do it!

Chloé
